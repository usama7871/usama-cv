# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all people, regardless of background or identity. We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior Examples
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Being patient and understanding
- Asking questions before making assumptions
- Giving credit and recognizing contributions

### Unacceptable Behavior
- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate
- Discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, etc.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Threats of violence or violent language

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community interactions including:
- GitHub issues and discussions
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Commit messages
- Email communication
- Social media interactions
- Any official project spaces

## Reporting & Enforcement

### How to Report
If you witness or experience a Code of Conduct violation:

1. **Document the incident**: What happened, when, where, who was involved
2. **Report privately**: Email kusamakhan1234@gmail.com
3. **Include context**: Links, screenshots, or additional information
4. **Be specific**: Provide as much detail as possible

### Reporting Template
```
From: [Your Name]
Subject: Code of Conduct Report

Incident Description:
[What happened]

Parties Involved:
[Who was involved]

When:
[Date and time]

Where:
[Link to conversation or location]

Why it violates the Code:
[Explain the violation]

Evidence:
[Screenshots, links, etc.]

Impact:
[How did this affect you or the community]
```

### Response Timeline
- **24 hours**: Acknowledgment of report
- **3 days**: Initial investigation
- **7 days**: Decision and action
- **Ongoing**: Follow-up and support

### Privacy
All reports are reviewed confidentially. We will:
- Protect your identity
- Not disclose details without consent
- Communicate outcomes appropriately
- Support all parties fairly

## Corrective Actions

Violations may result in:

### Level 1 (Minor)
- Warning and explanation
- Request for behavior change
- Mandatory reading of Code of Conduct
- 30-day probation

### Level 2 (Moderate)
- Temporary suspension from community (3-30 days)
- Apology requirement
- Behavioral agreement

### Level 3 (Severe)
- Permanent removal from community
- Account ban
- Report to GitHub Trust & Safety

## Appeals Process

Those subject to enforcement can appeal:

1. Submit written appeal within 14 days
2. Explain why the decision was unjust
3. Provide additional context or evidence
4. Appeals reviewed by independent reviewer
5. Final decision communicated within 7 days

## Examples

### ✅ Good Community Interactions

**Respectful Disagreement:**
```
"I see your point about using X, but I'm wondering if Y might have 
better performance. Let me share this benchmark I found. What do you think?"
```

**Constructive Feedback:**
```
"Good effort on this PR! I have a suggestion: could we optimize the 
image loading? Here's an approach I've found works well..."
```

**Inclusive Language:**
```
"Hey team, let's review this together" 
instead of 
"Hey guys, check this out"
```

### ❌ Problematic Interactions

**Dismissive:**
```
"That's a dumb idea" or "You clearly don't understand this"
```

**Exclusionary:**
```
"Real developers know X" or "This is too junior-level"
```

**Harassing:**
```
"Your code is terrible and you should feel bad"
```
